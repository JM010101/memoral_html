Our Beneficiaries

At the heart of the Fallen Gator initiative is a simple belief: support should be meaningful, timely, and respectful. The beneficiaries of this program are high school students who have been quietly identified by school administration as needing extra support to fully participate in school life.

Our focus is on the schools that historically fed into Grantham High School. These are the communities that shaped the Fallen Gators, and this is where their legacy continues. By directing support back into these feeder schools, we are keeping the impact close to home and rooted in the same hallways, classrooms, and neighbourhoods that once shaped us.

Students are not chosen through applications, publicity, or self-promotion. They are selected through trusted educators who see the challenges students face every day and understand where help can make a real difference. Our role is to provide support without judgement, labels, or exposure.

As the program rolls out, this page will share the types of support provided, not the names or identities of the students who receive it. Confidentiality matters. Dignity matters. The goal is not recognition. The goal is impact.

Types of Support Provided

Support may include assistance with school supplies, academic or extracurricular fees, educational trips, equipment, or other essential needs that allow students to stay engaged, confident, and included.

By focusing on outcomes rather than individuals, we ensure students receive help without being defined by their circumstances.
