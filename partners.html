Our Partners

The Fallen Gator initiative exists because people and organizations choose to step up when it matters. Our partners are corporations and government organizations that have made a conscious decision to support a cause rooted in dignity, remembrance, and meaningful impact.

These partners are not sponsors. They are not advertisers. They do not receive promotions, endorsements, or commercial benefit in return for their support. What they receive instead is something far more important: the knowledge that they are helping make a real difference for a good cause.

By joining the Fallen Gator mission, our partners help ensure that the legacy of fallen classmates lives on in a way that is practical and forward-looking. Their contributions support memorial efforts and fund quiet, targeted student support initiatives identified directly by school administration. These are needs that rarely make headlines but matter deeply to the students affected.

Our partners understand that the most meaningful acts of generosity are often the ones done without recognition. Their involvement reflects leadership, community responsibility, and a commitment to doing the right thing simply because it is the right thing to do.

We are grateful for every organization that chooses to stand with us. Their support strengthens the bridge between generations and turns remembrance into opportunity.

This page exists to acknowledge their commitment and say thank you.

Partner Donation

We invite companies and business partners who want to stand behind the Fallen Gators mission to contribute here.

Your donation supports our Silent Heroes cause, providing meaningful, behind-the-scenes assistance that honours former Grantham Gators while helping where it matters most.

The minimum partner donation is $50. If you wish to contribute more, simply adjust the quantity to reflect your level of support.

We are committed to responsible stewardship. 90% or more of every partner donation is directed to the Silent Heroes cause, with only minimal costs required to administer and sustain the program.

This is not about recognition or promotion. It's about showing up, doing the right thing, and supporting a mission rooted in respect, community, and remembrance.
